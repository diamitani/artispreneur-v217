What is a DSP (Digital Service Provider)?

Chapter 1

Module 1.1: What is a DSP (Digital Service Provider)? A Digital Service Provider (DSP) is an online platform that distributes music to streaming services, allowing your songs to be available on Spotify, Apple Music, Amazon Music, and other platforms. Key Points: ● DSPs handle the logistics of getting your music on multiple streaming services. ● You retain ownership of your music while DSPs distribute it for public consumption.
Module 1.2

Why You Need a Distribution Service

Chapter 1

Module 1.2: Why You Need a Distribution Service Using a DSP ensures your music reaches a wide audience: ● Exposure: DSPs allow you to distribute your music globally. ● Revenue: You earn royalties every time your music is streamed or downloaded. ● Convenience: DSPs handle the backend work, freeing you to focus on creating.

Step-by-Step Guide to Uploading Your Music

Chapter 3

Module 3.1: Step-by-Step Guide to Uploading Your Music 1. Sign Up: Create an account with your preferred DSP. 2. Upload Your Music: Ensure your tracks are properly mixed, mastered, and in the correct format (usually WAV or FLAC). 3. Module 3.2

Preparing Your Music for Distribution

Chapter 3

Module 3.2: Preparing Your Music for Distribution Make sure your music is ready for distribution by: ● Ensuring high-quality audio (WAV or FLAC files). ● Finalizing album artwork (at least 3000x3000 pixels). ● Collecting ISRC and UPC codes (some DSPs generate these for you).

Collecting Royalties from Streaming Platforms

Module 4.1: Collecting Royalties from Streaming Platforms Royalties from DSPs include: ● Mechanical Royalties: Earned from streams and downloads. ● Public Performance Royalties: Paid when your music is played on the radio or in public spaces. ● Publishing Royalties: Ensure you're collecting these if you write your own music.

Module 6.1: Can I Switch DSPs After I Release a Song? Yes, but it requires removing your music from one DSP and re-uploading it to another, which can disrupt streams and playlist placements.

Module 6.2: How Much Do DSPs Take in Fees? Each DSP takes a small percentage of royalties as a fee for distributing your music. Some take no additional fee, but charge per release or through subscriptions.
