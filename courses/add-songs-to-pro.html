How to Add Songs to Your PRO

Royalties20 lessons
Module 1.1

What is a P.R.O.?

Chapter 1

Module 1.1: What is a P.R.O.? A Performing Rights Organization (P.R.O.) collects public performance royalties on behalf of songwriters and publishers when their music is played on the radio, TV, live venues, or streaming platforms.
Module 1.2

Why Register Your Songs with a P.R.O.?

Chapter 1

Module 1.2: Why Register Your Songs with a P.R.O.? Registering your songs with a P.R.O. ensures: ● Royalties: You get paid whenever your music is publicly performed. ● Legal Protection: Your music is officially registered, providing proof of ownership.
Module 1.3

Overview of Major P.R.O.s (ASCAP, BMI, SESAC)

Chapter 1

Module 1.3: Overview of Major P.R.O.s (ASCAP, BMI, SESAC) The three major P.R.O.s in the U.S. are: ● ASCAP: Non-profit organization, widely used by independent artists. ● BMI: Similar to ASCAP, free to join for songwriters. ● SESAC: Invitation-only, but offers more personalized service. Chapter 2: How to Sign Up with a P.R.O.
Module 2.1

Choosing the Right P.R.O. for Your Music

Chapter 2

Module 2.1: Choosing the Right P.R.O. for Your Music Each P.R.O. has slightly different benefits, so it’s important to: ● Compare membership fees. ● Consider the ease of use and reporting systems. ● Look at the additional benefits they offer, like songwriter events or tools.
Module 2.2

Step-by-Step Guide to Signing Up

Chapter 2

Module 2.2: Step-by-Step Guide to Signing Up To sign up: 1. Visit the P.R.O. Website: Go to ASCAP, BMI, or SESAC’s website. 2. Create an Account: Fill in your personal and music-related information. 3. Agree to Terms: Read and agree to the membership terms.
Module 2.3

Costs and Benefits of P.R.O. Membership

Chapter 2

Module 2.3: Costs and Benefits of P.R.O. Membership ● ASCAP and BMI: Free for songwriters, small fee for publishers. ● SESAC: Invitation-only, so no public fee structure. Chapter 3: How to Add Songs to Your P.R.O. Account
Module 3.1

Registering Compositions with a P.R.O.

Chapter 3

Module 3.1: Registering Compositions with a P.R.O. Once registered as a member: ● Log in to your P.R.O. account. ● Add a new composition: Provide the title, genre, and details about the work. ● Include Co-Writers: Add splits for co-writers, making sure percentages add up to 100%.
Module 3.2

Adding Co-Writers and Splits

Chapter 3

Module 3.2: Adding Co-Writers and Splits It’s crucial to correctly input: ● Each co-writer’s P.R.O. ID. ● Ownership percentages (typically 50/50 if there are two co-writers). ● Publisher Information: If you have a publishing deal, make sure your publisher is added.
Module 3.3

Understanding Song Ownership

Chapter 3

Module 3.3: Understanding Song Ownership Registering a song does not transfer ownership—it just ensures that royalties are collected and distributed according to the splits you set. Chapter 4: Claiming Tracks and Ensuring Proper Registration
Module 4.1

What to Do If Your Song Is Already Registered

Chapter 4

Module 4.1: What to Do If Your Song Is Already Registered If you discover that someone else has registered your song: ● Check the registration to see if it’s correct. ● Claim your portion if you’re listed as a co-writer.
Module 4.2

Claiming Performances and Radio Plays

Chapter 4

Module 4.2: Claiming Performances and Radio Plays P.R.O.s allow you to submit setlists and claim live performances: ● Submit live performance details for concerts or tours. ● Claim radio airplay if your music was played on broadcast or online radio.
Module 4.3

Avoiding Registration Errors

Chapter 4

Module 4.3: Avoiding Registration Errors Common mistakes include: ● Misspelled song titles. ● Incorrect splits. ● Leaving out co-writers. Double-check all information before submitting. Chapter 5: Collecting Royalties from Your P.R.O.
Module 5.1

Types of Royalties Paid by P.R.O.s

Chapter 5

Module 5.1: Types of Royalties Paid by P.R.O.s P.R.O.s pay public performance royalties, which include: ● Radio and TV airplay. ● Live performances. ● Streaming royalties from platforms like Spotify or YouTube.
Module 5.2

How to Ensure You’re Collecting Everything You’re Owed

Chapter 5

Module 5.2: How to Ensure You’re Collecting Everything You’re Owed ● Check quarterly statements for accuracy. ● Submit live performance reports to claim royalties from concerts or events.
Module 5.3

International Royalties and Sub-Publishing

Chapter 5

Module 5.3: International Royalties and Sub-Publishing If your music is played internationally, P.R.O.s collect royalties through reciprocal agreements with foreign organizations. Consider signing with a sub-publisher if you have significant overseas activity. Chapter 6: Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)
Module 6.1

How Long Does It Take for a Song to Be Registered?

Chapter 6

Module 6.1: How Long Does It Take for a Song to Be Registered? Typically, it takes 1–2 weeks for a song to be processed and appear in your P.R.O. account.
Module 6.2

What Happens If I Don’t Register My Songs?

Chapter 6

Module 6.2: What Happens If I Don’t Register My Songs? If you don’t register your songs, you won’t collect performance royalties, even if your music is played on the radio or in public.
Module 6.3

How Do I Change or Update Registration Information?

Chapter 6

Module 6.3: How Do I Change or Update Registration Information? You can update registration details by: ● Logging into your account. ● Editing the song’s information (title, splits, etc.). ● Contact your P.R.O. if you need help correcting an error. Chapter 7: Course Conclusion and Next Steps

Recap of Key Points

How to Keep Your Catalog Updated

Module 7.2: How to Keep Your Catalog Updated Regularly update your P.R.O. account with new songs and correct any errors in older registrations to maximize your royalty collection.
