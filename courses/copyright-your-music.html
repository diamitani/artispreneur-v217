Module 1.1

What is a Copyright?

Chapter 1

Module 1.1: What is a Copyright? ● Definition: A copyright is a legal right granted to creators of original works, including music compositions and sound recordings. ● Key Points: ○ Copyright protection applies to literary, musical, artistic, and other works. ○ Once a work is copyrighted, the creator has the exclusive right to use, distribute, and license that work. ○ Music creators can copyright both the composition (musical notes, lyrics) and the sound recording (actual recorded performance).

Composition Copyright

Chapter 2

Module 2.1: Composition Copyright ● Definition: A composition copyright protects the written music (melodies, lyrics, and other musical elements). ● Key Points: ○ Applies to the underlying musical composition. ○ Often held by songwriters and composers. ○ Necessary if you intend to license your music for cover songs, sheet music, or film scores.
Module 2.2

Sound Recording Copyright

Chapter 2

Module 2.2: Sound Recording Copyright ● Definition: This copyright protects the actual recorded performance of the composition. ● Key Points: ○ Applies to the recorded version of a song (the master recording). ○ Often held by the recording artist or record label. ○ Essential for streaming, distributing on digital platforms, and licensing the actual recording for use in media.

Module 3.2

Step-by-Step Guide to Registering Your Music for Copyright

Chapter 3

Module 3.2: Step-by-Step Guide to Registering Your Music for Copyright ● Step 1: Visit the U.S. Copyright Office Website. ○ Link to: https://www.copyright.gov/registration/ ● Step 2: Choose the correct form (Form SR for sound recordings, Form PA for compositions). ● Step 3: Complete the registration form with details about the work you are copyrighting. ● Step 4: Pay the filing fee (typically $35 for a single work). ● Step 5: Submit your work, either digitally or physically, to the U.S. Copyright Office.
