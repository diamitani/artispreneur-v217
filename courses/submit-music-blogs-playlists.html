How to Submit Music to Blogs & Playlists

Marketing23 lessons
Module 1.1

Why Submitting to Blogs and Playlists is Important

Chapter 1

Module 1.1: Why Submitting to Blogs and Playlists is Important Blogs and playlists are essential for promoting independent music because they: ● Boost Exposure: Playlists and blogs help your music reach new audiences. ● Add Credibility: Being featured on a reputable blog or playlist builds your reputation as an artist. ● Drive Streams: Playlist placements often result in an increase in streams, which can boost your overall visibility.
Module 1.2

How Blogs and Playlists Help Grow Your Audience

Chapter 1

Module 1.2: How Blogs and Playlists Help Grow Your Audience By getting featured: ● Blogs: Write-ups on music blogs can attract dedicated listeners and potentially lead to interviews, reviews, or even playlist placements. ● Playlists: Playlist curators have a large following, which gives your song the chance to be heard by more people.
Module 1.3

Understanding What Blogs and Playlists Look For

Chapter 1

Module 1.3: Understanding What Blogs and Playlists Look For To increase your chances of being featured: ● Quality of Music: High-quality, well-produced music has the best chance. ● Presentation: Professionalism matters. Have a clear, concise pitch with all the necessary materials (streaming links, press kits, bio). ● Relevance: Make sure your music matches the genre and style of the blog or playlist you’re submitting to. Chapter 2: How to Find Blogs and Playlists for Your Genre
Module 2.1

Researching Blogs in Your Niche

Chapter 2

Module 2.1: Researching Blogs in Your Niche Use directories like Hype Machine and Indie Shuffle to find blogs that cover your genre. Search for: ● Genre-Specific Blogs: These cater to your specific style (indie, hip-hop, electronic, etc.). ● Emerging Artist Blogs: These platforms are designed to showcase up-and-coming talent.
Module 2.2

Finding Playlist Curators on Spotify, Apple Music, and YouTube

Chapter 2

Module 2.2: Finding Playlist Curators on Spotify, Apple Music, and YouTube Look for independent curators and user-generated playlists by: ● Searching Playlist Keywords: Use genre or mood-based keywords on Spotify or YouTube. ● Engaging with Curators on Social Media: Many playlist curators are active on Instagram, Twitter, or LinkedIn and may post submission guidelines there.
Module 2.3

Creating a Target List of Blogs and Playlists

Chapter 2

Module 2.3: Creating a Target List of Blogs and Playlists Organize a target list of relevant blogs and playlists in a spreadsheet: ● Blog/Playlist Name. ● Contact Information. ● Submission Guidelines (if available). ● Date of Submission. Chapter 3: Preparing Your Music for Submission
Module 3.1

Key Elements of a Professional Music Submission

Chapter 3

Module 3.1: Key Elements of a Professional Music Submission Before submitting, ensure you have: ● A High-Quality Track: Only submit your best, fully mastered music. ● Streaming Links: Provide direct links to Spotify, Apple Music, or YouTube (avoid attachments). ● Professional Artwork: Eye-catching visuals that align with your brand.
Module 3.2

How to Write a Compelling Pitch

Chapter 3

Module 3.2: How to Write a Compelling Pitch Your pitch should be: ● Personalized: Address the recipient by name and mention why your music is a good fit for their blog or playlist. ● Brief but Detailed: Include a short bio, your music’s unique qualities, and a clear call to action.
Module 3.3

Including Press Kits, Social Proof, and Streaming Links

Chapter 3

Module 3.3: Including Press Kits, Social Proof, and Streaming Links Attach or link to a digital press kit (EPK) that includes: ● Bio: Short, engaging story about you as an artist. ● Press Mentions: Include any previous blog features, interviews, or quotes. ● Social Proof: Highlight notable streaming numbers, social media followers, or collaborations. Chapter 4: Submitting Your Music to Blogs
Module 4.1

How to Submit to Music Blogs via Email

Chapter 4

Module 4.1: How to Submit to Music Blogs via Email When submitting via email: ● Subject Line: Make it compelling but concise. ● Introduction: Introduce yourself briefly, mention the blog by name, and explain why your music is a good fit. ● Body: Provide streaming links, a short bio, and relevant info.
Module 4.2

Using Blog Submission Platforms (SubmitHub, Hype Machine)

Chapter 4

Module 4.2: Using Blog Submission Platforms (SubmitHub, Hype Machine) Platforms like SubmitHub and Hype Machine allow artists to: ● Submit their music directly to blogs and playlists. ● Track submissions and see feedback from curators. ● SubmitHub: Offers both free and paid submission options.
Module 4.3

Best Practices for Writing Your Blog Submission Email

Chapter 4

Module 4.3: Best Practices for Writing Your Blog Submission Email Keep your email: ● Polite and professional. ● Focused: Stay on topic and get to the point quickly. ● Link-Friendly: Avoid attachments, instead provide direct streaming links. Chapter 5: Submitting Your Music to Playlists
Module 5.1

How to Reach Out to Playlist Curators

Chapter 5

Module 5.1: How to Reach Out to Playlist Curators When contacting playlist curators: ● Find Their Contact Information. ● Personalize Your Email: Mention why your track is a great fit for their playlist.
Module 5.2

Using Playlist Submission Platforms (Playlist Push, Daily Playlists)

Chapter 5

Module 5.2: Using Playlist Submission Platforms (Playlist Push, Daily Playlists) Platforms like Playlist Push or Daily Playlists allow you to: ● Submit your music to a large network of playlist curators. ● Increase your chances of placement on curated lists.
Module 5.3

How to Increase Your Chances of Playlist Placement

Chapter 5

Module 5.3: How to Increase Your Chances of Playlist Placement To increase your chances: ● Submit your track early. ● Provide context: Explain what your song is about and why it suits their playlist. ● Follow guidelines. Chapter 6: Following Up with Blogs and Playlist Curators
Module 6.1

When and How to Follow Up

Chapter 6

Module 6.1: When and How to Follow Up Follow up after: ● 7–10 days if you haven’t received a response. ● Keep your follow-up brief and polite, referencing your original submission.
Module 6.2

Avoiding Spammy Follow-Ups

Chapter 6

Module 6.2: Avoiding Spammy Follow-Ups Don’t bombard blogs or curators with too many emails. Respect their time.
Module 6.3

Building Long-Term Relationships with Curators

Chapter 6

Module 6.3: Building Long-Term Relationships with Curators Maintain relationships by: ● Sending updates when you release new music. ● Thanking them for any features or placements. ● Supporting their work by promoting their blogs or playlists on your social media. Chapter 7: Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)
Module 7.1

How Many Blogs or Playlists Should I Submit To?

Chapter 7

Module 7.1: How Many Blogs or Playlists Should I Submit To? Start by submitting to 20–30 blogs or playlists that match your genre.
Module 7.2

What If I Don’t Get a Response?

Chapter 7

Module 7.2: What If I Don’t Get a Response? Don’t be discouraged if you don’t get a response. Many curators receive hundreds of submissions.
Module 7.3

Should I Pay for Submissions?

Chapter 7

Module 7.3: Should I Pay for Submissions? Some services, like SubmitHub, charge for guaranteed feedback. Focus on organic submissions first. Chapter 8: Course Conclusion and Next Steps
Module 8.1

Recap of Key Points

Chapter 8

Module 8.1: Recap of Key Points ● Targeted Submissions. ● Professional Pitches. ● Follow-Up.
Module 8.2

How to Stay Organized and Track Your Submissions

Chapter 8

Module 8.2: How to Stay Organized and Track Your Submissions Use a spreadsheet or CRM tool to: ● Track the blogs and playlists you’ve submitted to. ● Monitor responses and follow-up dates. ● Keep notes on each interaction.
