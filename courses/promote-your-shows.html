How to Book and Promote Your Own Shows

Marketing16 lessons
Module 1.1

Why Book Your Own Shows?

Chapter 1

Module 1.1: Why Book Your Own Shows? Booking your own shows gives you: ● Control over your performance schedule. ● The ability to choose venues that align with your audience. ● Experience in managing your career directly.
Module 1.2

Benefits of Self-Promotion

Chapter 1

Module 1.2: Benefits of Self-Promotion Self-promotion helps you: ● Build your brand and audience. ● Gain valuable experience in marketing. ● Increase your visibility and fanbase. Chapter 2: Finding and Contacting Venues
Module 2.1

Researching Venues

Chapter 2

Module 2.1: Researching Venues When researching venues: ● Consider the size and type of venue that fits your music style. ● Check out other artists’ shows to see what venues might be a good fit.
Module 2.2

Contacting Venue Owners and Bookers

Chapter 2

Module 2.2: Contacting Venue Owners and Bookers When reaching out: ● Send a professional email with your press kit. ● Be clear about your availability, expected draw, and type of performance. Chapter 3: Preparing for Your Show
Module 3.1

Creating a Press Kit

Chapter 3

Module 3.1: Creating a Press Kit A press kit should include: ● A professional bio. ● High-quality photos. ● Links to your music and videos. ● Contact information.
Module 3.2

Setting a Budget

Chapter 3

Module 3.2: Setting a Budget When budgeting: ● Include costs for venue rental, promotion, travel, and equipment. ● Estimate potential income from ticket sales and merchandise.
Module 3.3

Promotion Strategies

Chapter 3

Module 3.3: Promotion Strategies Promote your show by: ● Using social media ads. ● Creating event pages. ● Posting flyers and reaching out to local press. Chapter 4: Day of the Show
Module 4.1

Soundcheck and Setup

Chapter 4

Module 4.1: Soundcheck and Setup On show day: ● Arrive early for soundcheck. ● Make sure all equipment is working properly. ● Coordinate with the sound engineer.
Module 4.2

Engaging with the Audience

Chapter 4

Module 4.2: Engaging with the Audience During your show: ● Interact with the audience to create a memorable experience. ● Encourage fans to follow you on social media and join your mailing list. Chapter 5: After the Show
Module 5.1

Following Up with Venues

Chapter 5

Module 5.1: Following Up with Venues After your performance: ● Thank the venue owners and staff. ● Ask for feedback and discuss future opportunities.
Module 5.2

Collecting Feedback from Fans

Chapter 5

Module 5.2: Collecting Feedback from Fans Gather fan feedback by: ● Asking for comments on social media. ● Encouraging fans to share photos and videos from the show.
Module 5.3

Reviewing Your Performance

Chapter 5

Module 5.3: Reviewing Your Performance Review your show by: ● Watching video recordings. ● Reflecting on what went well and what could be improved. Chapter 6: Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)
Module 6.1

What If I Can’t Afford to Rent a Venue?

Chapter 6

Module 6.1: What If I Can’t Afford to Rent a Venue? Consider alternatives like house concerts, community centers, or partnering with other artists to share costs.
Module 6.2

How Do I Handle Low Ticket Sales?

Chapter 6

Module 6.2: How Do I Handle Low Ticket Sales? ● Promote harder in the days leading up to the event. ● Offer discounts or bundle deals with merchandise. ● Focus on delivering a great performance to those who attend. Chapter 7: Course Conclusion and Next Steps
Module 7.1

Recap of Key Points

Chapter 7

Module 7.1: Recap of Key Points ● Booking and promoting your own shows helps you gain control and experience. ● Preparation and promotion are key to success.
Module 7.2

Moving Forward with Your Live Music Career

Chapter 7

Module 7.2: Moving Forward with Your Live Music Career Continue booking shows, expanding to new venues, and building a loyal fanbase through consistent performances.
