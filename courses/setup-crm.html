Business20 lessons
Module 1.1

What is a CRM and Why Do Artists Need It?

Chapter 1

Module 1.1: What is a CRM and Why Do Artists Need It? A Customer Relationship Management (CRM) tool helps you organize and manage your contacts in one place. For artists, it’s essential for: ● Tracking relationships with music blogs, playlist curators, and venues. ● Organizing fans, collaborators, and industry professionals.
Module 1.2

Benefits of Using a CRM

Chapter 1

Module 1.2: Benefits of Using a CRM Using a CRM allows you to: ● Keep track of communication with industry contacts. ● Schedule follow-ups and tasks to maintain relationships. ● Centralize your database of emails and phone numbers.

Overview of Popular CRM Platforms

Chapter 1

Module 1.3: Overview of Popular CRM Platforms Popular CRM platforms include: ● HubSpot: Free, with robust contact management features. ● Zoho CRM: Affordable and easy to use for small teams. ● Pipedrive: Offers visual pipelines to track your deals and communications. Chapter 2: Setting Up Your CRM

How to Import Contacts into a CRM

Chapter 2

Module 2.1: How to Import Contacts into a CRM Most CRMs allow you to import contacts via CSV files. You can export contacts from your email marketing platform or manually input key contacts.
Module 2.2

Creating Contact Segments (Fans, Blogs, Playlists, Venues)

Chapter 2

Module 2.2: Creating Contact Segments (Fans, Blogs, Playlists, Venues) Segment your contacts into categories for more targeted communication: ● Fans: Manage fan engagement for tours and releases. ● Blogs/Press: Track your media relationships and outreach. ● Venues: Keep track of past bookings and future inquiries.
Module 2.3

Organizing Your CRM for Maximum Efficiency

Chapter 2

Module 2.3: Organizing Your CRM for Maximum Efficiency Use tags, filters, and custom fields to make it easy to find contacts. For example: ● Tag venues based on city or genre. ● Label playlists based on their streaming platform. Chapter 3: Choosing the Right CRM Platform
Module 3.1

Overview of Free Platforms (HubSpot, Zoho, etc.)

Chapter 3

Module 3.1: Overview of Free Platforms (HubSpot, Zoho, etc.) HubSpot: Offers free contact management and email tracking. Zoho CRM: Free for small teams, with powerful automation tools. Capsule CRM: Focuses on ease of use, free for up to 250 contacts.
Module 3.2

Features to Look For in a CRM

Chapter 3

Module 3.2: Features to Look For in a CRM Consider the following features when choosing a CRM: ● Contact Management: Ability to store and manage contacts. ● Task Management: Set reminders and tasks for follow-ups. ● Automation: Automate repetitive tasks like follow-up emails.
Module 3.3

Paid CRM Alternatives

Chapter 3

Module 3.3: Paid CRM Alternatives If you need more advanced features, paid CRMs offer: ● Salesforce: Robust but costly, with a focus on large businesses. ● Pipedrive: Offers visual sales pipelines to track outreach. Chapter 4: Managing Your Contacts with a CRM

Tracking Communication with Blogs, Playlists, and Venues

Chapter 4

Module 4.1: Tracking Communication with Blogs, Playlists, and Venues Use your CRM to track every interaction with blogs, playlist curators, and venues: ● Log each communication and keep notes on responses. ● Set reminders for future follow-ups.
Module 4.2

Scheduling Follow-Ups and Reminders

Chapter 4

Module 4.2: Scheduling Follow-Ups and Reminders Most CRMs allow you to: ● Set reminders for each contact to follow up at the right time. ● Schedule recurring tasks for venues or playlists you regularly contact.
Module 4.3

Using Tags and Filters to Organize Your Database

Chapter 4

Module 4.3: Using Tags and Filters to Organize Your Database Tags allow you to organize your contacts based on categories, like: ● Genre: For blogs or playlists that cater to a specific style of music. ● Location: For venue contacts in specific cities.
