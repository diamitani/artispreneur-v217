What Are Collaborative Playlists?

Chapter 1

Module 1.1: What Are Collaborative Playlists? Collaborative playlists allow multiple users to: ● Add songs. ● Curate together, creating a shared experience.

Why Collaborative Playlists Are Important for Artists

Chapter 1

Module 1.2: Why Collaborative Playlists Are Important for Artists Collaborative playlists are important because they: ● Increase exposure: Allow others to help share your music with their audiences. ● Create opportunities for networking: Connect with other artists and fans. Searching for Playlists on Spotify and Other DSPs

Module 2.1: Searching for Playlists on Spotify and Other DSPs Use: ● Search terms like "collaborative playlist" or specific genre terms on Spotify or YouTube to find playlists you can join. ● Third-party websites like PlaylistExchange or Reddit threads dedicated to music curation.

Best Practices for Pitching Your Music

Chapter 3

Module 3.1: Best Practices for Pitching Your Music When submitting to playlists: ● Follow submission guidelines closely. ● Write a compelling pitch explaining why your song fits.

Following Playlist Guidelines for Submission

Chapter 3

Module 3.2: Following Playlist Guidelines for Submission Ensure you: ● Respect the playlist curator's rules (some may limit the number of songs per artist). ● Avoid spam submissions: Engage with the playlist first, then make your pitch. Module 6.1: How Do I Ensure My Song Stays on a Playlist? Ensure your song fits the playlist's theme, engages listeners, and follows the playlist's rules.
