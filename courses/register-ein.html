Module 1.1

What is an E.I.N.?

Chapter 1

Module 1.1: What is an E.I.N.? An Employer Identification Number (E.I.N.) is a unique number issued by the IRS to identify businesses for tax purposes. It is essential for independent artists who want to establish their venture as a legitimate business entity. The E.I.N. functions like a Social Security Number but is specifically for businesses.
Module 1.2

The Importance of an E.I.N. for Artists

Chapter 1

Module 1.2: The Importance of an E.I.N. for Artists For independent artists, an E.I.N. is critical for: ● Business Legitimacy: Establishes your creative work as a formal business entity in the eyes of the law and financial institutions. ● Financial Autonomy: Allows you to manage income, royalties, and expenses under a business name instead of your personal Social Security Number. ● Legal Protection: Provides the foundation for protecting your intellectual property and assets under a recognized business entity.
Module 1.3

E.I.N. vs. Social Security Number (SSN)

Chapter 1

Module 1.3: E.I.N. vs. Social Security Number (SSN) Many independent artists confuse the use of their Social Security Number with an E.I.N. However, the two serve different purposes: ● SSN: Used for personal tax filings and identifying individuals. ● E.I.N.: Used for business tax filings, registering a business, and managing business operations.
