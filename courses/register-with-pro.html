What is a Performing Rights Organization (P.R.O.)?

Chapter 1

Module 1.1: What is a Performing Rights Organization (P.R.O.)? A Performing Rights Organization, or P.R.O., is an entity responsible for collecting and distributing royalties to music creators when their songs are publicly performed. Public performances include plays on the radio, TV, streaming services, live venues, and even commercials.
Module 1.2

Key Roles of P.R.O.s in the Music Industry

Chapter 1

Module 1.2: Key Roles of P.R.O.s in the Music Industry ● License Issuance: P.R.O.s issue licenses to broadcasters, venues, and digital platforms. ● Royalties Collection: They collect royalties for the public use of music, ensuring that artists get paid. ● Royalty Distribution: P.R.O.s distribute the royalties collected to songwriters, composers, and publishers.
Module 1.3

Overview of Major P.R.O.s

Chapter 1

Module 1.3: Overview of Major P.R.O.s ● BMI: Known for being a low-cost option, favored by independent artists. ● ASCAP: A membership-driven organization with a reputation for supporting songwriters. ● SESAC: Invite-only, known for personalized service. ● SoundExchange: Handles digital performance royalties for streaming/satellite radio.
